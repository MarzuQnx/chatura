Our Practice

Navigating Indonesia's Energy Transformation

Indonesia's energy and natural resources sector stands at a critical transformation point. The global shift toward clean energy, combined with evolving sustainability demands, requires companies to fundamentally overhaul their operational and financial strategies. From traditional hydrocarbon operations to emerging renewable energy ventures, every player in the value chain faces unprecedented regulatory complexity and capital allocation challenges.

Chatura combines deep industry expertise with commercial insight to guide safe investments, efficient capital structuring, and regulatory risk mitigation across the full energy value chain. Our team understands the nuances of energy project economics, the intricacy of Indonesian mining and energy regulations, and the financial engineering required to make energy transition projects bankable.

Whether you are an established oil and gas operator navigating carbon disclosure requirements, a renewable energy developer structuring project finance, or a mining company seeking ESG compliance, Chatura delivers the integrated advisory services required to thrive in Indonesia's evolving energy landscape.

The Challenge

Key Challenges in Energy & Resources

Global Commodity Market Volatility

Fluctuating commodity prices create uncertainty in revenue forecasting, capital planning, and long-term investment decisions across the energy value chain.

Tight Regulatory Adjustments & Decarbonization

Evolving environmental regulations, carbon pricing mechanisms, and decarbonization mandates demand continuous compliance monitoring and strategic adaptation.

Capital Restructuring for Green Transition

Shifting from fossil fuel operations to clean energy requires complex capital restructuring, project financing, and strategic reallocation of resources.

Environmental Compliance & Carbon Reporting

Meeting increasingly stringent environmental standards and carbon emission reporting requirements across Indonesian and international frameworks.

Complex Cross-Border Investment Structures

Multinational energy operations require navigating complex transfer pricing, treaty networks, and cross-border investment structuring to optimize tax efficiency.

Workforce Transition & Safety Requirements

Energy transition demands workforce reskilling while maintaining rigorous operational safety standards across hazardous environments and remote locations.

Our Solution

Integrated Energy Advisory Practice

Our Integrated Energy Advisory Practice combines financial precision with sector expertise to navigate the energy transition. We deliver transaction advisory, project financing, tax optimization, and regulatory compliance across the full energy value chain.

From structuring renewable energy project finance and optimizing tax incentives for clean energy investments to ensuring environmental regulatory compliance, we provide the integrated expertise Indonesian energy companies need to transition sustainably and profitably.

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Chatura serves clients across the full energy value chain including oil and gas exploration and production, upstream and downstream operations, renewable energy (solar, wind, geothermal, hydroelectric), mining and minerals, power generation and distribution, and energy infrastructure development.

We provide end-to-end advisory for renewable energy projects including project financing structuring, feasibility financial modeling, tax incentive optimization under Indonesian renewable energy regulations, regulatory licensing support, and ESG compliance advisory aligned with international sustainability frameworks.

Energy companies in Indonesia may access several tax incentives including Investment Tax Allowance (ITA), Super Deduction for vocational training, accelerated depreciation for energy infrastructure, and specific incentives for renewable energy projects under Government Regulation No. 9/2016. Our tax team evaluates your eligibility and structures applications to maximize benefits.

We adopt a practical, compliance-first approach to ESG in energy. This includes carbon emission reporting aligned with Ministry of Environment regulations, sustainability disclosure preparation per OJK requirements, environmental impact assessment advisory, and alignment with Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) frameworks for listed energy companies.
